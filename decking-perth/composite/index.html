What Is Composite

Composite Decking Explained

Composite decking boards are manufactured from a blend of recycled wood fibres (typically 40-60%) and high-density polyethylene (HDPE) polymer. The result is a board that has the look and weight of real timber but with none of timber's weaknesses — no rotting, no splintering, no warping, and no termite vulnerability.

Modern composite boards feature co-extruded caps — a protective polymer shell that wraps around the core on all four sides. This cap layer provides UV resistance, stain resistance, and scratch resistance far beyond what raw timber or even first-generation composites could achieve. The surface texture replicates natural timber grain so convincingly that most people cannot tell the difference once installed.

The boards are hollow-core or solid-core depending on the brand and application. We recommend solid-core boards for high-traffic areas and pool surrounds where the added density provides a more solid feel underfoot. All composite systems use hidden clip fixings, eliminating visible screws for a clean, seamless finish.

Built for Perth

Why Composite Decking Works in Perth

No Splinters

This is the number one reason Perth families choose composite for pool decks and entertaining areas. Kids running barefoot, wet feet from the pool, pets — nobody is pulling splinters out at the end of the day. The smooth, consistent surface stays safe year after year without degrading.

No Oiling or Sanding

A hardwood deck in Perth needs oiling at least once a year — usually twice if you want it to look good. That means sanding back, cleaning, and spending a weekend applying oil. Composite decking needs none of this. A hose-down with soapy water once or twice a year is all the maintenance required.

Fade Resistant

Perth gets some of the highest UV levels in the world. Untreated timber greys out within months. Even well-maintained hardwood gradually loses colour between oiling cycles. Premium composite boards use UV-stabilised pigments in the co-extruded cap layer, holding their colour for the life of the product — backed by manufacturer warranties of 15 to 25 years.

Termite Proof

WA has some of the most active termite populations in Australia. Timber decking in contact with the ground — or even close to it — is an invitation for subterranean termites. Composite decking contains no organic material that termites can consume. Your deck and, by extension, your home are safer.

Slip Resistant

Quality composite boards are tested and rated for wet slip resistance, making them ideal around pools and in areas that catch irrigation overspray. The textured surface provides grip even when soaking wet — which is exactly what you need for a pool deck in Perth where the deck-to-pool transition happens a hundred times every summer.

Sustainable Choice

Most composite boards are manufactured from recycled materials — recovered timber waste and recycled plastics that would otherwise end up in landfill. You get a deck that lasts 25+ years and reduces demand on old-growth timber forests. At end of life, composite boards are themselves recyclable.

Honest Comparison

Composite vs Timber Decking — The Full Picture

We install both composite and hardwood decking, so we have no bias here. This is an honest breakdown to help you decide what is right for your situation.

Factor Composite Hardwood Timber
Annual Maintenance Hose-down only Sand + oil every 12 months
Lifespan 25+ years 15-20 years (if maintained)
Splinters None — ever Develops over time, especially when dry
Colour Fade Minimal (UV-stabilised) Significant without regular oiling
Termite Risk Zero Moderate to high (WA conditions)
Heat Retention Can get warm in direct sun Generally cooler underfoot
Upfront Cost Higher per m2 Lower per m2
10-Year Total Cost Lower (no maintenance spend) Higher (oil + labour costs compound)
Natural Look Very close — textured grain Authentic natural timber

The most common concern about composite decking is heat retention in direct sun. Modern composites have improved significantly, but in Perth's 40-degree peak summer days, a dark-coloured composite board in full sun will be warm underfoot. We address this by recommending lighter colour options for sun-exposed pool decks and pairing decks with our insulated patio structures for shaded sections.

Where cost is a deciding factor, composite has a higher per-metre material cost than hardwood. However, when you factor in hidden clip fixings (included), no finishing costs at install, and zero annual maintenance spend over the deck's life, composite typically costs less over a ten-year period than a well-maintained hardwood deck.

Styles & Colours

Popular Composite Decking Styles

We work with leading Australian composite decking brands that offer a wide range of timber-look colours and textures. Here are the most popular finishes our Perth clients choose.

Spotted Gum Look

Warm mid-tones with subtle grain variation. The most popular composite colour in Perth — suits both modern and traditional homes.

Walnut / Merbau Look

Rich, dark brown tones that replicate the look of oiled Merbau. Best in shaded or covered areas where heat retention is not a concern.

Coastal Grey

A weathered, silvery finish that suits coastal properties in Hillarys, Mindarie, and Ocean Reef. Hides sand and stays cooler than darker tones.

Charcoal

Bold, contemporary look. Pairs well with light-rendered homes and Colorbond Monument fencing. We recommend this for covered decks under patios.
